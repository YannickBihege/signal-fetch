## Framework

The Signal measures every position not in nominal currency terms, but in ounces of gold. By pricing equities against gold spot, we strip out the distortions of monetary inflation and currency fluctuation, leaving a cleaner read of whether a stock is genuinely appreciating in real purchasing-power terms. A position that rises in USD but falls in gold-ounce terms is, by this measure, losing ground.

Entry and exit discipline is guided by the 14-period Relative Strength Index (RSI-14) applied to each position's USD price:

| RSI-14 Range | Signal |
|---|---|
| Below 40 | **BUY** — oversold, consider initiating or adding |
| 40 – 70 | **HOLD** — momentum neutral, maintain existing position |
| Above 70 | **NO ENTRY** — overbought, avoid new capital deployment |

## This week

ASML's gold-denominated value of 0.3308 oz remains the portfolio's largest real-terms holding, but its decline underscores how even quality names are not immune to the current risk-off rotation.

The macro backdrop continues to be shaped by a persistently strong gold price, which at $4,539.90 signals that institutional demand for hard-asset hedges remains elevated. The EUR/USD rate at 1.1628 reflects a dollar that has softened relative to the euro, which mechanically compresses the EUR-denominated prices of these USD-listed equities and is a meaningful headwind for European-based investors tracking this portfolio. OKLO, the sole nuclear energy name in the portfolio, saw the sharpest drawdown at -7.38%, consistent with the higher-beta, speculative-growth profile of early-stage energy infrastructure stocks in a week when appetite for risk faded.

OKLO's trajectory will continue to hinge on regulatory progress with the Nuclear Regulatory Commission and broader U.S. energy policy signals.
