## Framework

The Signal measures every position not in nominal currency terms but in ounces of gold. This approach strips out the noise of monetary inflation and currency fluctuation, anchoring portfolio performance to a hard, politically neutral store of value. A position is only genuinely appreciating if it is gaining ground against gold — not merely rising in dollars or euros.

Entry and exit discipline is guided by the 14-period Relative Strength Index (RSI-14), applied as follows:

| RSI-14 Range | Signal |
|---|---|
| Below 40 | **BUY** — oversold, consider initiating or adding |
| 40 – 70 | **HOLD** — momentum is constructive; maintain existing positions |
| Above 70 | **NO ENTRY** — overbought, do not add; review existing exposure |

No new capital is deployed into positions registering above 70. Existing holdings in the NO ENTRY zone are not automatically sold, but are monitored closely for mean-reversion signals.

## This week

Semiconductor equities staged a broad rally this week, with the portfolio's chip-related names — TSM, NVDA, and AMD — all posting meaningful nominal gains. AMD's near-14% advance is the standout, apparently driven by stronger-than-anticipated forward guidance and renewed enthusiasm around AI accelerator demand. In gold terms, however, even these sharp nominal moves look more modest: AMD's 0.0739 oz valuation reflects that gold itself continues to command a historically elevated price, now clearing $4,700 per ounce. The hard-money lens is doing its job, reminding us that nominal gains and real wealth accumulation are not the same thing.

The macro backdrop remains one of persistent dollar softness, with EUR/USD holding above 1.17. That environment is generally constructive for gold and puts ongoing pressure on USD-denominated asset values when translated back to the euro base. ASML, reporting in euros, sidesteps some of this currency friction and its measured +2.81% gain — with RSI sitting comfortably in the mid-50s — represents one of the more balanced risk/reward profiles in the book this week. The Dutch lithography leader continues to benefit from structural semiconductor capex commitments that remain largely insulated from short-term macro volatility.

OKLO is the outlier, declining 7.14% this week and the only position in the red. The advanced nuclear developer is inherently high-volatility, and this week's pullback likely reflects the broader risk-off rotation within speculative clean-energy names rather than any fundamental deterioration. At an RSI of 61.1, the position remains in HOLD territory — not yet oversold enough to add to — and merits patience. Its gold valuation of 0.0151 oz underscores that this is a high-risk, small-allocation satellite position and should be sized accordingly.
