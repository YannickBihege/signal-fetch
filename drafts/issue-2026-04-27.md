## Framework

The Signal measures every position not in nominal currency terms but in ounces of gold. By denominating prices against gold spot — currently $4,681.89 / €3,984.92 per troy ounce — we strip out the inflationary noise embedded in fiat exchange rates and assess whether a holding is genuinely appreciating in real purchasing-power terms. A stock that rises 5% in euros while gold rises 8% has, in our framework, lost ground.

Entry and exit discipline is guided by the 14-period Relative Strength Index (RSI-14):

| RSI-14 Range | Signal |
|---|---|
| Below 40 | **BUY** — oversold, look to accumulate |
| 40 – 70 | **HOLD** — trend intact, no new capital deployed |
| Above 70 | **NO ENTRY** — overbought, avoid adding exposure |

## This week

Semiconductor equities are once again driving the bulk of portfolio-level volatility, with NVDA posting a strong +4.0% nominal advance and OKLO surging nearly +7.0% — the latter likely buoyed by continued positive sentiment around small modular reactor licensing timelines and growing data-centre power-demand narratives. Against a gold spot price that remains elevated above $4,680, however, nominal gains must be interpreted cautiously: in gold-denominated terms, all five positions remain modest fractions of an ounce, and real outperformance requires equity prices to climb faster than gold itself.

The macro backdrop is defined by a stubbornly strong gold price, which continues to reflect persistent institutional demand, central-bank accumulation, and residual uncertainty around global trade policy and currency stability. EUR/USD at 1.1749 provides a slight tailwind for European-listed or euro-denominated investors holding USD-priced assets, though the relationship can reverse quickly given current policy divergence between the Federal Reserve and the ECB. ASML's modest −1.73% decline is worth monitoring given its role as a bellwether for the broader semiconductor capital-equipment cycle; the HOLD zone reading of RSI 51.8 suggests the pullback is orderly rather than indicative of a structural breakdown.

Looking ahead, the primary risk to this portfolio is a continuation of the momentum run in NVDA and AMD pushing those names further into overbought territory while gold holds firm, compressing their oz-gold valuations even as headline prices look attractive. Earnings reports from major technology companies expected through the coming weeks may serve as a catalyst in either direction. Discipline around the RSI framework is especially important in this environment — the temptation to chase momentum in NVDA and AMD should be resisted until RSI conditions reset.
